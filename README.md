# Computational & Neurotechnology-Based Framework for Early Cognitive Impairment Detection

**IEEE Conference Paper — Accepted for Publication, 2026**  
**Oral Presentation — INCIP 2026**  
Manipal University Jaipur

---

##  Overview

Cognitive impairment conditions such as Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) and early-stage dementia often go undetected until significant neurological damage has occurred. This paper proposes a computational and neurotechnology-based framework that leverages **multimodal neuroimaging data** and **machine learning algorithms** for early and accurate detection of cognitive impairment.

---

##  What This Paper Does

- Conducts a **systematic literature review** following PRISMA guidelines across PubMed, Scopus, and Web of Science
- Analyzes the integration of **neuroimaging techniques** (structural, behavioral, psychometric signals) with ML models
- Evaluates commonly used **predictive modeling approaches** and evaluation methodologies in cognitive impairment research
- Identifies key challenges: **data heterogeneity, model generalization, and clinical interpretability**
- Proposes future directions integrating **advanced learning algorithms with multimodal neuroimaging frameworks**

---

##  Key Topics Covered

- Neuroimaging (MRI, fMRI, PET) + ML fusion
- CNNs, LSTMs, and Transformer-based models for cognitive assessment
- Multimodal data integration strategies
- PRISMA-based systematic review methodology
- AI interpretability in clinical diagnostics
